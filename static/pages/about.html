Takedown Observer

About

Takedown Observer is an open dataset that documents which accounts have been withheld at the country-level from the X/Twitter platform in response to legal takedown orders from various countries.
This research tool seeks to uncover the extent of geo-blocking, wherein a website utilises a visitor’s geographic location to withhold access to certain content available elsewhere.
The data is collected through an accompanying browser extension, which automatically detects if an account is withheld in a particular country when a user encouters a block page. This information is available through X’s API which shows when an account has been withheld in response to a government order and lists the countries that are affected. The extension reports blocked accounts to a database that is made openly available through this website.
The browser extension is recommended for use by researchers looking to quickly confirm and collate user reports of account takedowns on X and is available on Github.
Takedown Observer is a project of the internet Research Lab. It was developed by Divyank Katira with support from ARTICLE 19's Internet of Rights Fellowship.
